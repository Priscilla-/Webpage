Research

A few active threads from my work.

Gravitational-wave inference

Fast and reliable parameter estimation for compact binary coalescences, including reduced-order methods, surrogate likelihoods, and robust pipelines for realistic detector data.

Eccentric compact binaries

Detection and classification of orbital eccentricity using wavelet scattering transforms and lightweight classifiers, with attention to degeneracies with precession and modelling systematics.

Scientific machine learning

Structured neural networks, flow matching, and hybrid physics-informed approaches for inverse problems and dynamical systems where data alone should not be allowed to run the kingdom.

Reduced-order quadrature

Developing efficient quadrature rules for gravitational-wave likelihoods, including randomized low-rank ideas and multiresolution representations that preserve physical fidelity while cutting computational cost.

Inverse problems and imaging

Methods for recovering information from noisy, blurred, or incomplete observations, ranging from regularisation-based image restoration to mathematically grounded learning approaches.
