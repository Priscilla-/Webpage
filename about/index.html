About

I am an applied mathematician working on gravitational-wave astronomy, inverse problems, and scientific machine learning. My research focuses on building methods that combine mathematical structure with modern data-driven tools, especially for problems where signals are faint, models are expensive, and uncertainty matters.

I am affiliated with the University of Cambridge and the Alan Turing Institute. My work often lives in the productive borderlands between theory and computation: waveform modelling, statistical inference, structured neural architectures, and efficient surrogates for scientific discovery.
